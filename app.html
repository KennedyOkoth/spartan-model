1. Core Prediction Targets and Market Requirements

1.1 Match Outcome Prediction

Classification Accuracy & Probability Calibration

The foundation rests on accurate classification of match outcomes into three discrete categories: home victory, draw, or away victory. Draws typically occur in 25-30% of matches across European leagues, creating structural skew that models must address through careful sampling strategies or class-weighted loss functions.

Key Insight

Research demonstrates that ensemble approaches combining multiple base learners can achieve classification accuracies ranging from 55.9% to 62.7% on validation datasets, with the upper bound approaching theoretical limits of predictability given football's stochastic nature.

Algorithm Performance Comparison
Multinomial Logistic Regression 59.55% 497
Random Forest 55.9-62.7% 439
XGBoost (stacked) 61-67% 97
Neural Network (MLP) 86.7% 246

Probability Calibration for Betting Edge

Probability calibration represents the critical bridge between model outputs and actionable betting decisions. The GAP (Goals, Attempts, Possession) framework developed at LSE explicitly incorporates odds-implied probabilities as features, enabling direct comparison between model forecasts and market expectations.

This architecture demonstrated sustained profitability over 12-year backtesting, with average returns of 0.8% per bet when employing maximum available odds. 520

1.2 Score and Goal Predictions

Exact Scoreline Forecasting

Exact score prediction represents the most granular forecasting objective, with 16-25 common scorelines accounting for majority outcomes. The Betfair tutorial advocates separate regression models for home and away goals, with joint distribution reconstruction through independence assumption or copula-based dependence modeling. 72

Poisson regression provides theoretical foundation with count-data support, yet empirical goal distributions exhibit overdispersion that negative binomial or zero-inflated specifications better accommodate.

Over/Under 2.5 Goals Market

The over/under 2.5 goals market commands particular attention due to exceptional liquidity and extensive historical data. The LSE GAP research specifically targeted this market, demonstrating that incorporating shot and corner statistics alongside goals improved predictive performance over goal-only approaches.

Bundesliga: 2.9 avg goals, ~55% over 2.5 261
Serie A: 2.5 avg goals, ~47% over 2.5 282

Both Teams to Score (BTTS) Probability

The BTTS market offers attractive properties: binary outcomes, moderate baseline rates (50-55%), and substantial liquidity. Prediction requires joint estimation of each team's scoring probability against specific opposition, with positive correlation in open, attacking matches raising BTTS probability above independence predictions.

PerformanceOdds research indicates first-half BTTS rates exceed 70% when both teams demonstrate strong first-half performance indicators, suggesting in-play modeling improvements. 382

Corner prediction has emerged as a particularly promising niche market due to lower bookmaker attention and strong statistical regularity. The futbol_corners_forecast project achieves MAE < 2.0 corners per match—approximately 15-25% relative error on typical totals (9-11 corners).

6. Profitability and Betting Strategy Integration

6.1 Edge Detection and Value Betting

Probability-to-Odds Comparison

The fundamental betting operation calculates Expected Value: EV = p × o - 1, where positive EV requires p > 1/o. The margin of edge determines stake sizing through Kelly criterion variants.

Value Betting Example
Model Probability
45%
Market Odds
2.50 (40% implied)
Edge & EV
+5pp, +12.5%

Market efficiency varies: major league 1X2 markets are highly efficient (small edges), while secondary markets like corners show greater inefficiency (10-20% edges achievable). 268

Kelly Criterion & Stake Sizing

Kelly criterion formula: f = (bp - q) / b, where b = odds - 1, p = win probability, q = 1 - p. Fractional Kelly addresses probability estimation uncertainty.

Full Kelly
Extremely aggressive, high variance
Half Kelly (0.5×)
Practical standard, moderate variance
Quarter Kelly (0.25×)
Conservative, low variance approach

6.2 Risk Management Framework

Bankroll Allocation Principles

Diversification across uncorrelated or negatively correlated markets reduces portfolio variance. Mean-variance optimization or Kelly extension to multiple simultaneous bets provides principled frameworks.

Goals ↔ Corners +0.3 to +0.4 correlation
Goals ↔ Cards Context-dependent
Corners ↔ Cards Weak correlation
Different matches ~0 correlation

Correlation-Aware Position Sizing

Explicit correlation modeling prevents hidden concentration risk through covariance estimation from historical prediction residuals.

Position Adjustment Example

Over 2.5 goals + Over 9.5 corners: Reduce to 70-80% of full stake each due to positive correlation, maintaining proper risk exposure.

Optimal weighting depends on error correlation structure, with negatively correlated errors enabling risk reduction through diversification.

Drawdown Limits & Abandonment Criteria

Criterion Threshold Action

Weekly loss limit 10% of bankroll Intensive review, potential update
Monthly loss limit 20% of bankroll Strategy suspension, reassessment
Consecutive losses 10+ at expected 55% win rate Statistical process control check
CLV degradation Sustained negative CLV Model degradation signal, retraining

Statistical process control involves tracking prediction accuracy and returns against historical distributions, with automatic alerts for deviations exceeding 95% confidence intervals.

7.2 Model Training and Validation

Time-Series Cross-Validation

Strict temporal order preservation with expanding window validation, preventing information leakage through proper chronological ordering.

❌ Random k-fold

Never for time series - catastrophic future information leakage

⚠️ Blocked k-fold

Season-based blocks - moderate risk, exploratory only

✅ Time-series CV

Expanding window - low risk, hyperparameter tuning

⭐ Walk-forward

Production simulation - minimal risk, final validation

Implementation: sklearn TimeSeriesSplit or custom expanding window with strict chronological ordering and out-of-fold base model predictions

Hyperparameter Optimization

Bayesian optimization with Optuna or genetic algorithms for efficient search in complex, non-convex hyperparameter spaces common in football prediction.

Grid search Very high cost
Random search Moderate cost
Bayesian optimization Moderate cost, recommended
Genetic algorithms High cost, complex spaces
Optimization Objectives
Primary: MAE for regression, log-loss for classification
Secondary: Betting simulation validation for final selection
Constraints: Computational budget, training time limits

Complete Operational Backtesting

Complete simulation including prediction generation, edge identification, stake sizing, execution simulation, and performance tracking against historical closing odds with realistic assumptions.

Simulation Components
Prediction generation: Model inference with temporal integrity
Edge identification: Compare to closing odds with minimum threshold
Stake sizing: Kelly or fractional Kelly with bankroll assumptions
Execution simulation: Assume closing odds with slippage adjustment
Performance tracking: Cumulative return, drawdown, Sharpe ratio
Alternative Validation Approach
Closing Line Value (CLV) Analysis

Compare predictions vs. closing odds without waiting for outcomes— faster feedback, lower variance performance estimate.

Key Advantages
  • Faster feedback loop for model improvement
  • Lower variance than outcome-based validation
  • Direct market efficiency measurement
  • Early warning for model degradation

Critical requirement: Strict temporal integrity preventing lookahead bias, with proper handling of odds movement between prediction and execution time.
